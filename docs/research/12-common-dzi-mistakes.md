# Research: Most Common Mistakes on Bulgarian DZI Exam (Матура по БЕЛ)

## Summary of Research

This document compiles findings from web research on the most common mistakes Bulgarian students make on the DZI (Държавен зрелостен изпит) exam in Bulgarian Language and Literature (БЕЛ). Data is drawn from educational platforms, teacher blogs, Ministry of Education reports, and exam preparation centers.

---

## EXAM STRUCTURE CONTEXT

The DZI BEL exam is 240 minutes (4 hours), divided into 3 modules:
- **Module 1** (60 min): 23 questions -- closed and open answer (language norms, reading comprehension)
- **Module 2** (60 min): 17 questions -- closed and open answer (literature knowledge, text analysis)
- **Module 3** (120 min): 1 task -- writing an interpretive composition (ЛИС) OR literary essay (есе), worth 30 points

Total: 100 points. Average national score in 2024: **58.48 points (Good 4.32)**; in 2025: **57.53 points (Good 4.27)**.

## 1. SPELLING MISTAKES (Правописни грешки) -- HIGH FREQUENCY

### 1.1 Full vs. Short Definite Article (Пълен и кратък определителен член)
**THE single most tested and most failed area.**

| Rule | Example Correct | Example Incorrect |
|------|----------------|-------------------|
| Subject (подлог) takes FULL article: -ът/-ят | Ловец**ът** хвана глигана. | Ловец**а** хвана глигана. |
| Non-subject takes SHORT article: -а/-я | Видях ловец**а**. | Видях ловец**ът**. |
| After "съм" (to be) -- always FULL article | Той е победител**ят**. | Той е победител**я**. |
| Nicknames/epithets -- always SHORT article even as subject | Иван Грозн**и**я дойде. | (exception to main rule) |

**Nouns ending in -ар, -тел, -яр, -й take -ят/-я (not -ът/-а):**
- героят / героя (NOT героът / героа)
- писателят / писателя (NOT писателът / писатела)
- млекарят / млекаря (NOT млекарът / млекара)

**Exceptions that use -ът/-а despite ending in -ар:** сън, цар, лакът, нокът, път, огън, ден, зет, кон, крал.

### 1.2 Yat Vowel Alternation (Ятов преглас / Променливо Я)
**Second most problematic spelling area.**

Rule: The old Bulgarian letter ЯТ is written as **Я** when BOTH conditions are met:
1. The vowel is under stress (ударение)
2. The following syllable is NOT soft (no front vowels е, и after it)

Otherwise it is written as **Е**.

| Correct | Incorrect | Rule Applied |
|---------|-----------|-------------|
| мляко | млеко | stressed + hard syllable = Я |
| бял | бел | stressed + no following syllable = Я |
| бели (plural) | бяли | before soft syllable (и) = Е |
| пели (past tense) | пяли | before soft syllable (и) = Е |
| смях | смех | stressed + hard = Я |
| смешен | смяшен | before soft syllable (е) = Е |
| вятър | ветър | stressed + hard = Я |
| ветрове (plural) | вятрове | unstressed = Е |
| живял | живел | stressed + hard = Я |
| живели (plural) | живяли | before soft syllable (и) = Е |

### 1.3 Doubled Consonants (Удвоени съгласни)
When a prefix ends with the same consonant that starts the root, both are written:

| Correct | Incorrect | Structure |
|---------|-----------|-----------|
| бе**зз**ащитен | бе**з**ащитен | без + защитен |
| о**тт**ласквам | о**т**ласквам | от + тласквам |
| на**дд**елявам | на**д**елявам | над + делявам |
| въ**зз**елен | въ**з**елен | въз + зелен |
| радос**тт**а | радос**т**а | радост + та |
| есе**нн**и | есе**н**и | есен + ни |

### 1.4 "ЙО" vs "ЬО" Rule
- After a VOWEL: **ЙО** -- район, майонеза
- After a CONSONANT: **ЬО** -- шофьор, миньор

### 1.5 Plural with -ИИ (not -ИЙ)
- Correct: много зод**ии**, иници**ир**ах
- Incorrect: много зод**ий**

### 1.6 Comparison Forms (Степенуване)
- Correct: **по-**хубав, **най-**хубавото (WITH hyphen)
- Incorrect: по хубав, най хубавото (without hyphen)
- Correct: повече, по-малко (irregular -- no hyphen on повече)
- Incorrect: по-вече

### 1.7 Prepositions с/със and в/във
- Use **във** only before words starting with В or Ф: във влака, във Франция
- Use **в** before all others: в колата (NOT във колата)
- Use **със** only before words starting with С or З: със сол, със зимни гуми
- Use **с** before all others: с десен волан (NOT със десен волан)

### 1.8 Capitalization (Главни букви)
- Month/day names are LOWERCASE: 15 декември, понеделник (NOT 15 Декември)
- Second word in compound proper names is lowercase: Българско възраждане (NOT Българско Възраждане)
- Historical eras/movements: Ренесанс, Просвещение (capitalized)

### 1.9 Common Misspelled Words

| Incorrect | Correct | Root explanation |
|-----------|---------|-----------------|
| пропуск**в**ателен | пропускателен | root: пуск- |
| **в**заимствам | заимствам | no prefix "в" |
| об**в**зема | обзема | no extra "в" |
| случ**е**й | случай | root: случ-ай |
| по**д**тискам | потискам | prefix по-, not под- |
| анцу**н**г | анцуг | no "н" |
| ви**ш**о | висше | root: вис- |
| съж**е**лявам | съжалявам | root: жал- |
| двой**н**ствен | двойствен | no extra "н" |

## 2. GRAMMAR MISTAKES (Граматични грешки) -- HIGH FREQUENCY

### 2.1 Numeral Form (Бройна форма)
**One of the most confusing areas for students.**

Rules:
- For NON-PERSON masculine nouns after numerals: use бройна форма (-а ending): два стол**а**, пет прозорец**а**
- For PERSON masculine nouns after numerals: use plural + special numeral: петим**а** ученици (NOT пет ученика)

| Incorrect | Correct | Explanation |
|-----------|---------|-------------|
| десет нови компютр**и** | десет нови компютр**а** | non-person = бройна форма |
| пет прозорц**и** | пет прозорец**а** | non-person = бройна форма |
| няколко критери**и** | няколко критери**я** | non-person = бройна форма |
| петима учител**я** | петима учител**и** | person = plural (NOT бройна) |
| пет очевидец**а** | петим**а** очевидц**и** | person = plural + петима |
| четири ученик**а** | четирим**а** ученик**и** | person = plural + четирима |

### 2.2 Relative Pronouns (Който/Когото)
- **Който** = subject (who): Ученикът, **който** дойде... (the student who came)
- **Когото** = object (whom): Ученикът, **когото** видях... (the student whom I saw)
- Students frequently use когото where който is needed and vice versa.

### 2.3 Reflexive Possessive Pronoun (Свой/Своя vs Негов/Неговия)
- When the subject possesses: use **свой/своя/своите**: Той продаде **своите** акции.
- NOT: Той продаде **неговите** акции. (This implies someone ELSE's shares.)

### 2.4 Verb Endings: -м vs -ме (1st person plural)
- Correct: Ние ходи**м**, пише**м**, чете**м**
- Incorrect: Ние ходи**ме**, пише**ме**, чете**ме**
- The -ме ending is dialectal/regional, not standard literary Bulgarian.

### 2.5 Participle Agreement (Деепричастия)
The participle must refer to the SAME subject as the main verb:
- Correct: **Вървейки**, намерих монета. (Walking, I found a coin -- same subject: I)
- Incorrect: **Вървейки**, започна да вали. (Walking, it started raining -- different subjects)

## 3. PUNCTUATION MISTAKES (Пунктуационни грешки) -- HIGH FREQUENCY

### 3.1 Missing Comma Before Subordinate Conjunctions
The most common punctuation error. Comma is REQUIRED before:
- **че** (that): Знам**,** че ще дойде.
- **който/която/което/които** (who/which): Книгата**,** която прочетох...
- **когато** (when): Ще дойда**,** когато мога.
- **защото** (because): Отидох**,** защото исках.
- **ако** (if): Ще помогна**,** ако поискаш.
- **въпреки че** (although): Дойде**,** въпреки че беше болен.
- **доколкото** (insofar as): Доколкото ми е известно**,** той дойде.

### 3.2 Unnecessary Comma Before "и" (and)
- Incorrect: Отидох до магазина**,** и си купих хляб.
- Correct: Отидох до магазина и си купих хляб.
- EXCEPTION: Comma before "и" is needed when "и" connects clauses with DIFFERENT subjects or when "и" is repeated (и...и...и).

### 3.3 Missing Comma After Introductory/Parenthetical Phrases
- Correct: Доколкото ми е известно**,** отрова не съществува.
- Incorrect: Доколкото ми е известно отрова не съществува.

### 3.4 Enclosing Parenthetical Elements with Commas on BOTH Sides
- Correct: Д-р Цончев**,** заявява пред Димитров**,** че...
- Incorrect: Д-р Цончев заявява пред Димитров, че...
- Participial clauses mid-sentence need commas on both sides:
  - Correct: отрова**,** предизвикваща инфаркт**,** не съществува
  - Incorrect: отрова, предизвикваща инфаркт не съществува

### 3.5 Location Phrases -- No Comma
- Correct: При нас в Полша имаме изпити.
- Incorrect: При нас**,** в Полша имаме изпити.
- Same: тук в България, там в Европа (no comma)

### 3.6 Missing Comma Before Second Part of Double Conjunctions
Comma required before the second element:
- или...или, дали...или, нито...нито, както...така и, не само...но и
- Не само четеше**,** но и пишеше.

## 4. LEXICAL MISTAKES (Лексикални грешки) -- MEDIUM-HIGH FREQUENCY

### 4.1 Paronyms (Пароними) -- Most Commonly Confused Pairs

| Often confused | Actual meanings |
|---------------|----------------|
| **ефектен** / **ефективен** | impressive / efficient |
| **икономичен** / **икономически** | thrifty (person) / economic (relating to economy) |
| **дипломатичен** / **дипломатически** | tactful / diplomatic (official) |
| **практичен** / **практически** | practical (person) / practical (relating to practice) |
| **компания** / **кампания** | company/group / campaign |
| **преписвам** / **приписвам** | to copy / to attribute |
| **превързвам** / **привързвам** | to bandage / to attach emotionally |
| **пренасям** / **принасям** | to carry / to sacrifice/offer |
| **параметър** / **периметър** | parameter / perimeter |
| **фактура** / **фрактура** | invoice / fracture |
| **нотариален** / **натурален** | notarial / natural |
| **ларви** / **лаври** | larvae / laurels |
| **индиец** / **индианец** | Indian / Native American |

### 4.2 Pleonasms (Плеоназми) -- Redundant Word Pairs
Students frequently use redundant expressions:
- ~~малки микрофирми~~ (small micro-firms -- "micro" already means small)
- ~~първи приоритети~~ ("first priorities" -- priority already means first)
- ~~друга алтернатива~~ ("other alternative" -- alternative already means other)
- ~~нови иновации~~ ("new innovations" -- innovation already means new)
- ~~кръгла сфера~~ ("round sphere" -- sphere is already round)
- ~~връх на апогея~~ ("peak of the apex" -- redundant)
- ~~сложен и компликиран~~ (same meaning repeated)
- ~~удобен и комфортен~~ (same meaning repeated)

### 4.3 Wrong Preposition Usage
- Incorrect: отивам **на** София -- Correct: отивам **в** София
- Incorrect: води **на** Левски -- Correct: води **срещу** Левски
- Incorrect: питам един въпрос -- Correct: **задавам** един въпрос

## 5. ESSAY WRITING MISTAKES (Грешки при писане на съчинение/есе) -- CRITICAL, HIGHEST POINT IMPACT

### 5.1 Grading Criteria Breakdown (30 points total)

**I. Literary Competencies (Литературни компетентности) -- max 10 points**
1. Understanding the conditional nature of literature: 0-2 pts
2. Knowledge of literary history, author, and work: 0-2 pts
3. Applying knowledge of literary structure/function to explain problems: 0-3 pts
4. Interpreting artistic meanings per stated problem: 0-3 pts

**II. Argumentative Text Competencies (Аргументативен текст) -- max 12 points**
1. Thesis matching the formulated problem: 0-3 pts
2. Adequate, concrete, exhaustive argumentation: 0-3 pts
3. Logical sequence of arguments: 0-3 pts
4. Well-defined, logically connected composition parts: 0-3 pts

**III. Language Competencies (Езикови компетентности) -- max 8 points**
1. Lexical norm: 0-2 pts
2. Grammatical norm: 0-2 pts
3. Spelling norm: 0-2 pts
4. Punctuation norm: 0-2 pts

### 5.2 Most Common Essay Mistakes (ranked by severity/frequency)

**CRITICAL: Retelling Instead of Analyzing (преразказ вместо анализ)**
- Students unintentionally retell the literary text instead of interpreting it
- They cannot distinguish between interpretation and summarization
- Argumentation is displaced by paraphrasing or excessive quoting
- This can cost up to 15-20 points across all literary and argumentative criteria

**CRITICAL: Missing or Weak Thesis (липса на теза)**
- Students write "everything they know" without a clear central argument
- The thesis does not correspond to the formulated problem
- Costs up to 3 points directly, but cascades -- without a thesis, argumentation falls apart (another 3-6 points)

**HIGH: No Logical Transitions (липса на логически преходи)**
- Missing connections between structural elements
- Missing transitions between sub-theses in the main body
- Sentences are disconnected, reading like a list rather than an argument

**HIGH: No Connection Between Examples and Arguments**
- Examples are cited but not linked back to the thesis
- Literary quotes are dropped in without analysis of their function

**HIGH: Poor Structure**
- No clear introduction / body / conclusion
- Body is not divided into coherent paragraphs with sub-arguments
- Missing conclusion that ties back to the thesis

**MEDIUM: Insufficient Literary Knowledge**
- Not knowing the work well enough to cite specific scenes/quotes
- Weak understanding of author's historical/cultural context
- Inability to discuss literary devices (metaphor, symbolism, etc.)

**MEDIUM: Weak Vocabulary / Register**
- Using colloquial language instead of academic register
- Influence of foreign languages on Bulgarian expression
- Declining reading habits lead to impoverished vocabulary

### 5.3 Performance Data on Essays
- Average essay score: **12 out of 30 points** (40%)
- Average interpretive composition score: **14 out of 30 points** (47%)
- 91% of students attempted the writing task in 2022/2023
- Original thinking criterion: only **3 points** out of 30 -- structurally underincentivized
- Students can get "Excellent" (6) overall grade despite mediocre essay, if multiple-choice is strong

## 6. LITERATURE KNOWLEDGE GAPS

### 6.1 Exam Syllabus
The DZI BEL literature component covers 9 thematic circles and ~27 authors/works from grades 11-12. Key authors include:
- Hristo Botev, Ivan Vazov, Aleko Konstantinov, Pencho Slaveykov, Peyo Yavorov, Elin Pelin, Yordan Yovkov, Dimcho Debelyanov, Geo Milev, Nikola Vaptsarov, Dimitar Dimov, Dimitar Talev, Yordan Radichkov, Blaga Dimitrova, etc.

### 6.2 Common Knowledge Gaps (based on teacher reports)
- Students delay reading the actual works, relying on summaries
- Weak knowledge of literary-historical context (movements, periods)
- Confusion between Romanticism / Realism / Modernism characteristics
- Inability to discuss specific scenes with textual evidence
- Shallow understanding of symbolism and imagery
- Memorizing pre-written essays instead of understanding the material -- one student scored 5.50 with a memorized composition, but this strategy fails when the topic doesn't match

### 6.3 Works Students Find Most Difficult (based on frequency of complaints)
- Works from the period of Bulgarian Modernism (Yavorov, Debelyanov, Geo Milev)
- Longer prose works that students skip reading (Dimitar Dimov "Тютюн", Dimitar Talev "Железният светилник")
- Poetry requiring close reading and symbolic interpretation

## 7. TIME MANAGEMENT FAILURES

### 7.1 Exam Time Structure
| Module | Duration | Content |
|--------|----------|---------|
| Module 1 | 60 min | 23 questions (language norms) |
| Module 2 | 60 min | 17 questions (literature) |
| Module 3 | 120 min | 1 essay/composition |
| **Total** | **240 min** | |

### 7.2 Common Time Mistakes
- **Spending too long on Module 1-2**: Students obsess over tricky multiple-choice and leave insufficient time for the essay (worth 30% of total score)
- **Not planning the essay before writing**: Students jump into writing without outlining, leading to disorganized text and wasted time on rewrites
- **Cannot leave during first 60 min of Module 3**: Students who finish early are trapped, creating anxiety
- **Not budgeting draft vs. final copy time**: The 120 minutes for Module 3 must cover planning, drafting, revising, and writing the final version

### 7.3 Recommended Time Allocation for Module 3 (120 min)
- 10-15 min: Read topic, plan thesis, outline structure
- 60-70 min: Write draft
- 20-30 min: Revise for content, logic, transitions
- 15-20 min: Final copy + proofread for spelling/punctuation

## 8. OFFICIAL MON DATA & TEACHER/GRADER PERSPECTIVES

### 8.1 Ministry of Education (МОН) Statistics

**2024 DZI BEL Results:**
- Average: 58.48 points (Good 4.32) -- highest on record
- Perfect scores (100 pts): 24 students
- Excellent (6): over 6% of grades
- Weak/failing (2): 4,325 students
- 98% of BEL exam questions test understanding and application (not memorization)

**2025 DZI BEL Results:**
- Average: 57.53 points (Good 4.27)
- Nearly twice as many perfect 100-point scores compared to previous years
- Top-performing regions: Sofia-city, Smolyan, Varna

### 8.2 Teacher/Evaluator Perspectives

**Key criticism from evaluators (source: Sega newspaper):**
- "Original thinking gets only 1 criterion worth 3 points" -- the system rewards memorization over creativity
- Students learn to avoid independent thinking, instead memorizing teacher-provided model answers
- Keyword-based grading: "If the key word is absent, even with a logically correct answer, no points are awarded"
- Teachers themselves often don't know the detailed grading criteria, leading to misaligned classroom preparation

**Teacher Stanislava Doktorova (137 SU "Angel Kanchev"):**
- Students rush through reading instructions without understanding what is asked
- "Conditions aren't like TikTok videos -- read carefully, underline requirements"
- Students cannot distinguish between spelling vs. grammar errors on the test
- Recent exams have fewer multiple-choice and more open-ended responses -- guessing is no longer viable

**EduHub.bg analysis (4,200+ exam papers analyzed):**
- Limited class hours in grades 11-12 prevent sufficient practice
- Students delay preparation until too late
- Studying multiple foreign languages diminishes native Bulgarian proficiency
- Daily use of foreign words causes difficulty expressing ideas in Bulgarian
- Declining interest in reading books leads to weak writing/speaking

## 9. PRIORITIZED MISTAKE LIST (by impact on score)

### TIER 1 -- CRITICAL (directly costs 5+ points)
1. **Retelling instead of analyzing** in essay -- cascading loss across literary + argumentative criteria
2. **Missing/weak thesis** -- loses 3 pts directly + 3-6 pts in argumentation
3. **Full vs. short article (членуване)** -- tested in multiple questions + essay
4. **Missing commas before subordinate conjunctions** (че, който, когато, etc.) -- tested + essay
5. **No logical structure in essay** -- loses 3 pts + readability penalties

### TIER 2 -- HIGH IMPACT (costs 2-4 points)
6. **Ятов преглас errors** (Я vs Е) -- tested in 1-3 questions
7. **Бройна форма confusion** (person vs non-person) -- tested in 1-2 questions
8. **Paronym confusion** (ефектен/ефективен, etc.) -- tested + affects essay
9. **Poor argumentation** -- examples without analysis, disconnected claims
10. **Unnecessary comma before "и"** -- common in essay, penalized

### TIER 3 -- MEDIUM IMPACT (costs 1-2 points)
11. **Doubled consonants** (бе**зз**ащитен) -- tested in 1 question
12. **Reflexive pronoun свой vs негов** -- tested
13. **Verb ending -м vs -ме** -- tested
14. **Capitalization errors** -- tested
15. **Pleonasms and tautology** -- affects lexical norm score in essay
16. **Relative pronoun който vs когото** -- tested

### TIER 3 -- LOWER IMPACT but common
17. **Comparison form hyphens** (по-хубав, NOT повече)
18. **Preposition в/във, с/със** misuse
19. **Location phrase comma errors**
20. **Participle subject agreement (деепричастия)**
